Write the year down.
Then lock it.

Memolock is a small offline app for keeping one honest report per year: books read, places visited, habits tracked, and a note to your future self. Fill it in as you go, then lock it so it can't quietly get rewritten later.

How it works

One report for last year. Write as many as you remember.

01

Start the year

Create a report for the previous year. The year itself is set the moment you create it and can't be changed afterward.

02

Fill it in as you go

Track books, places, sleep, steps, and exercise, and summarize the year with a quote.

03

Lock it

When you're ready, lock the report. It stays readable, but it becomes read-only until January 1 of the following year.

The lock

Permanent, on purpose

Once a report is locked, it can't be edited or reopened for editing until the unlock date arrives. That's the deal: you write it once, honestly, and then you leave it alone.

What locking does Marks the report read-only on this device until January 1 of next year. It does not delete anything, and it does not touch reports from other years.

Storage

Stays on your device

Every report lives in one readable JSON file next to the app. There's no account to make, nothing tracked, and nothing sent anywhere.

Open the file in a text editor if you ever want to look under the hood: it's plain data, not a database you need Memolock to unlock.

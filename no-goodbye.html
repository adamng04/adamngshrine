Don't just say "goodbye".

Some people have troubles with manners on the internet. If you want to ask something, type the question immediately than just saying hi. You haven't really done anything wrong, but it's better to be courteous.

But what about saying goodbye? Some people end the conversation simply by saying either "thanks" or "goodbye". It's fine, but if the recipients were reprimanded on how to ask questions, maybe they deserve a warmer farewell, and vice versa.

Take Sally for example: She's a busy and easily agitated worker who doesn't really have time for people to say hi or hello. If you don't apply yourself, Sally might be upset at you. A better way to lighten her mood would be to say goodbye in a more thoughtful way:

  • 5:30 Sally: I know you've been helpful, but I can't redeem it. Sorry, I hope you find someone else more reliable next time.
  • 5:31 Anonymous Indian scammer: It's fine Sally, I hope you have a lovely day. See you around!

In this example, emphasizing the "lovely day" from your farewell can make a lot of difference, as it doesn't end the work day on a negative note. If the opposite is pressing people to ask questions, saying a pleasant goodbye is better for both parties' mental well-being.
